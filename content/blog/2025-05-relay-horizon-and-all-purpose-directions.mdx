## Four directions, one question

The question that sits underneath All Purpose is deceptively simple: what does a capable life actually look like, and how do the tools and culture a person surrounds themselves with shape the answer?

Consumer products, at their worst, answer that question on behalf of the user without ever asking it. They assume a lifestyle. They manufacture aspiration. They create dependency dressed up as empowerment. All Purpose starts from a different premise — that capability is something a person builds for themselves, and that the role of the product is to make that building feel possible, visible, and worth continuing.

Four directions have emerged from that premise: Relay, Horizon, All Purpose Music, and Made It Out. Each one addresses the question from a different angle. Together they form something that is starting to look less like a product suite and more like a coherent world.

## Relay — the connective tissue

Relay is the community and connection direction inside All Purpose. It exists because capability does not survive in isolation.

There is a fairly well-documented pattern in high-achievement communities where individuals gain traction, acquire skill, or make meaningful progress — and then find themselves structurally isolated from others doing the same. The communities they came from may not reflect where they are going. The communities they are moving towards may not yet feel like home. Relay is built for the gap in between.

The name is deliberate. A relay race is not just a collection of fast runners — it is a transfer mechanism. Something has to pass between people for the race to continue. Relay is interested in that transfer: the moment where someone further along passes something real to someone behind them, not as charity but as part of the same forward motion.

What this looks like in practice is not a social network in the conventional sense. Relay is not interested in follower counts or public performance of progress. It is interested in the smaller, more durable connections that form when people who are working towards something serious find each other through that work — and through the values that sit underneath it.

The community direction also does something important structurally for All Purpose as a whole. Products that exist purely in transactional utility — track this, log that, complete the next step — eventually feel mechanical. Relay gives the ecosystem a human centre. It is the direction most likely to produce the kind of belonging that makes users want to stay not because the product is good but because the people around it are worth being around.

## Horizon — aspiration made honest

Horizon is the lifestyle and aspiration direction. That description requires some care, because aspiration is a word that has been weaponised by consumer marketing to the point where it has almost stopped meaning anything.

Most lifestyle products sell a destination. Buy this, look like that, feel like this version of yourself that is easier and richer and more at ease than the one you actually inhabit. The gap between the advertisement and reality is considered a feature, not a bug — it keeps you reaching, which keeps you spending.

Horizon does not do that. The aspiration it works with is a more honest kind: the real distance between where someone is and where they are genuinely trying to get to, and the cultural objects, references, experiences and aesthetics that help make that distance feel navigable rather than crushing.

The distinction matters because it shapes what Horizon produces. Content under this direction is not aspirational in the sense of being unattainable or performative. It is aspirational in the sense of being directionally honest — here is what it looks like to be someone who takes their life seriously, who has standards, who moves with intention. Not a fantasy, but a legitimate north star.

Aesthetically, Horizon is the direction that most explicitly engages with how things look and feel — the visual language of a life being built rather than merely lived. It has a role in shaping what All Purpose looks like as a brand in the world, even for people who have not yet opened the app.

## All Purpose Music

Music is not a feature. It is not background content, not an engagement trick, not algorithmic filler to increase session time. Music is one of the few things that can actually accompany someone through meaningful effort — and in that sense it belongs inside a capability platform more naturally than people might initially assume.

All Purpose Music is the direction concerned with the sonic environment inside the ecosystem. This is not about building a streaming service or competing with catalogue. It is about understanding that the music someone works to, moves to, thinks to, is part of the material conditions of how well they do those things. Sound is infrastructure.

What the direction explores is curation with a specific editorial logic: music that is coherent with the All Purpose thesis. That means music that feels oriented forward, that carries a certain standard in its production and its intent, and that reflects the cultural range of the people All Purpose is built for. The genres and references that sit inside All Purpose Music are not arbitrary — they reflect genuine taste and genuine attention to the communities this ecosystem is meant to serve.

There is also something culturally significant about an institution that controls its own sonic identity from the inside out. Music direction, when it is done with care, becomes one of the most recognisable signatures a brand can have — not because it is branded in a heavy-handed way, but because people begin to associate a certain feel with a certain world.

## Made It Out

Made It Out is the direction that takes the most explicit interest in story — specifically, the stories of people who have moved from circumstances that were difficult or constraining into lives that are more expanded, more capable, more self-determined.

The phrase itself matters. It is not "made it" — which implies arrival, completion, an endpoint. It is "made it out" — which implies escape from something specific, and the beginning of a different kind of journey rather than the end of the last one. The distinction is important because it is honest about the fact that the people whose stories this direction tells are not at the finish line. They are in motion. They got out of something, and now they are figuring out what comes next.

Made It Out sits at the intersection of documentation and inspiration, but it tries to avoid the usual traps of both. Straight documentation of struggle can become suffering tourism — people's hard experiences consumed as content without genuine respect for their intelligence or where they are going. Straight inspiration can become saccharine, a commodification of resilience that papers over the reality of what overcoming something actually costs.

The direction tries to hold both honestly: the specificity of where someone came from, and the seriousness of where they are going. The people whose stories belong inside Made It Out are not victims and they are not heroes in the Hollywood sense. They are people exercising genuine capability in real conditions, which is the most interesting subject All Purpose can concern itself with.

## The ecosystem taking shape

It is worth being clear about what these four directions represent structurally, not just culturally.

All Purpose is not a single app with a marketing angle. It is a consumer ecosystem with a coherent thesis at the centre — that capability is the most interesting thing a person can develop, and that the products, culture, music, community and stories a person surrounds themselves with are either in service of that or working against it.

Relay, Horizon, All Purpose Music and Made It Out are all expressions of the same underlying logic. They address the thesis from different positions: connection, aspiration, environment, story. Each one is a legitimate standalone direction, but the reason they coexist inside All Purpose rather than spinning out as separate brands is that they are more useful together. A person who is finding their community through Relay, orienting themselves through Horizon, working in the sonic environment of All Purpose Music, and drawing something from the stories inside Made It Out — that person is inside a world with a consistent point of view. That is harder to build than a single app and more durable than any individual feature.

CheekyGains and Naira, the fitness and coaching products in the All Purpose orbit, operate at the more concrete, tactical end of the capability thesis — the daily practice, the accountability, the performance coaching. The four directions above operate at the cultural end. Together, they cover a meaningful arc from the specific ("I am training today") to the expansive ("I am building a particular kind of life").

## Culture as architecture

The thing that unifies these directions is a belief that consumer culture is architecture. It is not decoration. It does not sit outside the serious work of building a life — it shapes the conditions inside which that work either flourishes or gets slowly ground down.

Most consumer products treat culture as a distribution channel. Get the aesthetic right, get the right people associated with the brand, and the product will find its audience. All Purpose is trying to do something different: build culture that is genuinely useful, that reflects real values rather than manufactured ones, and that grows more coherent over time rather than fragmenting under the pressure to chase every new trend.

That is an ambition with a long timeline. May 2025 is not when it is completed. It is a point at which the four directions have enough shape to name, and enough logic behind them to move forward with confidence.

The consumer ecosystem is taking shape. The question it is oriented around — what does a capable life look like, and what helps? — remains the right one.
